Currently there are two approaches used to calculate (U–Th–Sm)/He ages using in situ methods. In the first (absolute) approach, the age is calculated from the measured concentrations of U, Th, (Sm) and He using the following equation:

He/V = 8 (137.88/138.88) (exp[λ238t]-1) U + 7 (exp[λ235t]-1) U /138.88 +
6 (exp[λ232t]-1) Th + 0.1499 (exp[λ147t]-1) Sm (1)

where U, Th and Sm are expressed in mol μm−3 or similar units, He is the molar abundance of helium released from the ablation pit (in moles) and V is the ablation pit volume (in μm3). Alternately, the pairwise dating approach can be used, where the concentrations and pit volumes are normalized to a mineral standard of known (U–Th)/He age.

The measurements of U, Th, Sm and the ablation pit volume have associated systematic errors. resochronometer groups these into a single calibration factor, κ:

κ = (He/V) / { 8 (137.88/138.88) (exp[λ238t]-1) U + 7 (exp[λ235t]-1) U /138.88 +
6 (exp[λ232t]-1) Th + 0.1499 (exp[λ147t]-1) Sm } (2)

κ is unknown but can be estimated by analyzing a standard of known (U–Th–Sm)/He age. By measuring a series of standard zircon and normalizing the sample measurements to those of the standards, the age function can be solved and all uncertainties can be accounted for.
